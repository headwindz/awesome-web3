On August 29, 2023, a federal judge **dismisses major portions** of the SEC's lawsuit against Coinbase, dealing a significant blow to the regulator's "regulation by enforcement" strategy and providing crucial clarity on what constitutes a security in crypto.

## The Case

**SEC's Claims (June 2023):**

- Coinbase operating as unregistered securities exchange
- Selling unregistered securities
- Operating as unregistered broker and clearing agency
- 13 tokens identified as securities

**Judge's Ruling:**

Judge Katherine Polk Failla dismisses:

- Claims that Coinbase Wallet constitutes broker activity
- Claims about Coinbase's staking program
- Several procedural arguments

But allows to proceed:

- Claims about exchange listing of alleged securities
- Questions about tokens' security status

## Why This Matters

**Precedent Setting:**

- First major court ruling on what constitutes a crypto security
- Clarifies that providing wallet technology â‰  broker activity
- Validates staking as distinct from securities offerings

**Industry Impact:**

- Boosts confidence in building compliant US crypto businesses
- Signals judges willing to push back on SEC overreach
- Provides roadmap for other companies fighting SEC

**SEC Strategy Questioned:**

- Highlights problems with "regulation by enforcement"
- Exposes contradictions in SEC's classification approach
- Pressures for clear rules rather than case-by-case litigation

## Market Reaction

Crypto markets rally on the news. Coinbase stock surges 15%. Industry leaders celebrate as a win for clear regulation and US innovation.

The ruling doesn't end the case but significantly weakens the SEC's position and emboldens the industry to fight for regulatory clarity.
