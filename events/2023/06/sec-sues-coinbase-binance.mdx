In June 2023, the **SEC filed lawsuits** against the two largest crypto exchanges‚Äî**Binance** (June 5) and **Coinbase** (June 6)‚Äîaccusing them of operating unregistered securities exchanges and selling unregistered securities, marking the **most aggressive regulatory assault** on crypto in US history.

## The Binance Lawsuit (June 5, 2023)

**What SEC Alleged:**

1. **Unregistered exchange**: Operating without proper licenses
2. **Unregistered broker**: Acting as intermediary illegally
3. **Unregistered clearing agency**: Settling trades improperly
4. **Unregistered securities**: Listing 12+ tokens that are securities
5. **Market manipulation**: Wash trading, fake volume
6. **Commingling funds**: Mixing customer and company assets
7. **Lying to regulators**: False statements to investigators

**The 136-Page Complaint:**

SEC claimed Binance:

- Made **billions** in illegal profits
- **CZ (Changpeng Zhao)** personally liable
- **BAM Trading** (Binance.US) also named
- **Extensive evidence** of fraud
- Seeking **penalties**, **disgorgement**, and **injunctions**

**The "Securities" Named:**

SEC explicitly called these tokens securities:

- **BNB** (Binance's own token)
- **BUSD** (Binance stablecoin)
- **SOL** (Solana)
- **ADA** (Cardano)
- **MATIC** (Polygon)
- **FIL** (Filecoin)
- **ATOM** (Cosmos)
- **SAND** (Sandbox)
- **MANA** (Decentraland)
- **AXS** (Axie Infinity)
- **CHZ** (Chiliz)
- **COTI**

This was **nuclear**: Declaring major altcoins are securities.

## The "Howey Test" Debate

**What Is the Howey Test?**

1938 Supreme Court case defining **securities**:

1. **Investment of money**
2. **In a common enterprise**
3. **With expectation of profit**
4. **From efforts of others**

**The Question:**

Do crypto tokens meet this test?

**SEC's View:**

- **Almost everything** is a security (except Bitcoin, maybe Ethereum)
- **Token sales** = securities offerings
- **Staking rewards** = investment contracts
- **DAO tokens** = securities
- **Utility tokens** = Still securities

**Crypto's View:**

- **Decentralized tokens** ‚â† securities (no "others")
- **Utility tokens** have use cases beyond profit
- **Staking** = network participation, not investment
- **Clear rules needed** before enforcement

**The Gray Area:**

- **Bitcoin**: Commodity (SEC agrees)
- **Ethereum**: Unclear (SEC hints commodity)
- **Everything else**: SEC says security
- **Stablecoins**: Depends who issues

No clear guidance. Just lawsuits.
