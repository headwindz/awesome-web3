---
title: China Bans Bitcoin (First Time) - Price Crashes 50%
tags: [china, regulation, ban, pboc, exchanges]
date: 2013-12-05
---

On December 5, 2013, the **People's Bank of China (PBOC)** prohibits Chinese financial institutions from handling Bitcoin transactions, triggering the first of many "China bans Bitcoin" panics. Bitcoin crashes from **$1,150 to $600** in days, marking the first major regulatory threat to crypto.

## The Ban

**PBOC's Announcement:**

- Bitcoin is "not a currency" with legal status
- Banks cannot provide Bitcoin services
- Payment processors banned from Bitcoin
- Businesses cannot price goods in Bitcoin
- Individuals can still trade (for now)

**Why China Acted:**

- Bitcoin rally from $100 to $1,150 in 2 months
- Chinese exchanges (BTC China) dominated global volume
- Capital flight concerns (wealthy Chinese buying BTC to move money)
- No government control over Bitcoin
- Competing with yuan's international aspirations

## Market Impact

**The Crash:**

- **Before:** Bitcoin at $1,150 (all-time high)
- **After announcement:** Drops to $600 in 3 days (-47%)
- **BTC China:** Trading volume collapses
- **Global panic:** First major regulatory FUD event
- **Recovery:** Took months to stabilize

**Trading Volume Shift:**

Before the ban:

- Chinese exchanges: 60-70% of global volume
- BTC China was world's largest exchange
- Chinese miners dominated hash rate

After:

- Volume shifted to Western exchanges
- Bitstamp, Coinbase gained share
- But miners stayed in China (cheap electricity)

## Why This Mattered

**First Regulatory Reality Check:**

This was crypto's first encounter with major government opposition:

- Showed Bitcoin was vulnerable to regulatory action
- Proved nation-states could impact markets significantly
- Created "China bans Bitcoin" as recurring FUD pattern
- But also proved Bitcoin couldn't be killed entirely

**The Pattern Begins:**

This 2013 ban started a cycle:

- **2013:** Banks banned from Bitcoin
- **2014:** Exchanges face increasing restrictions
- **2017:** ICO ban, exchange closures
- **2021:** Complete mining ban
- **2024+:** Still mostly banned

Each time: Price crashes, panic, then recovery.

## The Irony

**Despite The Ban:**

- Chinese miners controlled 65-75% of Bitcoin hash rate until 2021
- Chinese exchanges found workarounds (P2P, offshore)
- Chinese investors used VPNs and foreign exchanges
- China became Bitcoin mining capital (until 2021 ban)

**What China Couldn't Stop:**

- Individuals trading Bitcoin
- Miners operating (until 2021)
- Blockchain development
- Crypto innovation
- Bitcoin's growth

## Historical Context

**What Triggered It:**

The Nov 2013 Bitcoin rally was insane:

- **November 1:** $200
- **November 30:** $1,150
- **5.75x in one month**
- Chinese media frenzy
- BTC China became world's largest exchange

The Chinese government saw:

- Massive capital potentially leaving China
- No control mechanisms
- Speculative mania
- Challenge to monetary sovereignty

## The Global Reaction

**Initial Panic:**

- "Bitcoin is dead" headlines
- Fears of other countries following suit
- Questions about Bitcoin's viability
- Many early adopters sold

**Longer-Term Lessons:**

- Bitcoin survived government bans
- Decentralization meant no single point of failure
- China's ban didn't kill Bitcoin (price recovered to $20K by 2017)
- Showed importance of regulatory navigation

## What Happened Next

**2014-2016:** Cat and mouse game

- Chinese exchanges operated in gray area
- Used offshore structures
- P2P trading exploded
- Miners continued operations

**2017:** Escalation

- September: Complete exchange ban
- ICO ban
- Forced shutdown of all domestic exchanges

**2021:** Final crackdown

- Mining ban
- Complete crypto services ban
- Miners exodus to US, Kazakhstan
- End of Chinese mining dominance

## The Legacy

**"China Bans Bitcoin" Memes:**

This became crypto's most predictable FUD pattern:

- China announces Bitcoin ban/restriction
- Market panics and crashes
- Bitcoin recovers within weeks/months
- Repeat every 1-2 years

By 2021, the community joked: "China banning Bitcoin again?" became a meme.

**What We Learned:**

1. **No kill switch:** Even hostile governments can't kill Bitcoin
2. **Decentralization works:** Network survived losing largest market
3. **Regulatory risk is real:** But not fatal
4. **Buy the China FUD:** Became a trading strategy

**Market Psychology:**

The 2013 ban established that:

- Regulatory news drives short-term price action
- Long-term fundamentals matter more
- "This time it's different" is usually wrong
- FUD creates buying opportunities

## The Bigger Picture

**China's Real Goal:**

Not to kill crypto, but to:

- Control capital flows
- Prevent yuan circumvention
- Develop own digital currency (Digital Yuan)
- Prevent wealth from leaving China

**What Actually Happened:**

- Bitcoin became more global and resilient
- Mining decentralized (after 2021)
- Chinese investors found workarounds
- Western exchanges grew stronger

**The lesson:** Government bans test crypto's resilience. Bitcoin passed the test by not dying, proving the decentralized architecture works. Each "China bans Bitcoin" episode made the network stronger by forcing diversification.

This was the first of many times crypto faced regulatory pressure - and survived.
