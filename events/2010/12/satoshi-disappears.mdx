On December 12, 2010, **Satoshi Nakamoto** posts his final message to the Bitcoin forum and vanishes forever. The mysterious creator of Bitcoin transfers control to others and walks away from a project worth billions, leaving behind **one of technology's greatest unsolved mysteries**.

## The Final Post

**Satoshi's Last Public Message:**

December 12, 2010, on bitcointalk.org:

> "There's more work to do on DoS, but I'm doing a quick build of what I have so far in case it's needed, before venturing into more complex ideas. The build for this is version 0.3.19."

**Then silence.**

No explanation. No goodbye. Just... gone.

**Last Known Activity:**

- **April 2011:** Final private email to Gavin Andresen
- Content: "I wish you wouldn't keep talking about me as a mysterious shadowy figure, the press just turns that into a pirate currency angle. Maybe instead make it about the open source project and give more credit to your dev contributors; it helps motivate them."
- Then: Complete disappearance

Satoshi proved you can bootstrap a trillion-dollar system then disappear. Ultimate immaculate conception.

## The Mystery Deepens

**Who Was Satoshi?**

**Individual Candidates:**

**Hal Finney:**

- First Bitcoin recipient
- Cryptographer, cypherpunk
- Lived near Dorian Nakamoto (ironic?)
- ALS diagnosis 2009 (died 2014)
- Denied being Satoshi
- Writing style different
- Most plausible single person

**Nick Szabo:**

- Created Bit Gold (Bitcoin precursor)
- Writing style similar
- Denied repeatedly
- Backdated blog posts (suspicious)
- Strong candidate

**Dorian Nakamoto:**

- Real person named Satoshi Nakamoto
- Newsweek "outed" him (2014)
- Wrong guy (not a cryptographer)
- Denied involvement
- Poor guy harassed

**Craig Wright:**

- **Claims** to be Satoshi (2016+)
- Cannot prove it
- Multiple court cases
- Fabricated evidence
- Universally rejected by community
- "Faketoshi"

**Other Candidates:**

- Adam Back (Hashcash creator)
- Wei Dai (b-money creator)
- Many others speculated

**Group Theories:**

Maybe Satoshi was:

- **NSA/CIA:** Create trackable digital currency
- **Group of cryptographers:** Collective effort
- **Tech company:** Secret project
- **Academic team:** Research project

**The Evidence:**

- **Coding style:** Single person
- **Writing consistency:** One author
- **Perfect execution:** Genius-level work
- **Early mining patterns:** One miner
- **Conclusion:** Probably individual or small team
