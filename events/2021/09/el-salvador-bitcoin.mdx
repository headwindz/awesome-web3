On September 7, 2021, **El Salvador** makes history as the first country to adopt **Bitcoin as legal tender**, alongside the U.S. dollar. President Nayib Bukele's government launches the **Chivo wallet** and distributes $30 worth of Bitcoin to every citizen who downloads it.

## The Bitcoin Law

Passed by El Salvador's Legislative Assembly on June 9, 2021, the law states:

- Bitcoin becomes legal tender with liberatory power for all debts
- All businesses must accept Bitcoin if they have the technology
- Prices can be denominated in Bitcoin
- Tax payments can be made in Bitcoin
- Bitcoin-dollar exchanges are tax-exempt
- Government creates $150M Bitcoin trust for conversions

## The Infrastructure

**Chivo Wallet Launch:**

- State-developed Bitcoin/Lightning wallet
- $30 Bitcoin airdrop to incentivize adoption (~$150M total)
- 200+ Chivo ATMs installed nationwide
- Free conversion between BTC and USD
- No transaction fees for users

**Bitcoin Beach:**

- El Zonte village pilot program (started 2019)
- Circular Bitcoin economy proof-of-concept
- Anonymous donor funded local Bitcoin adoption
- Model for national rollout

**Volcano Bonds & Mining:**

- Plan for $1B Bitcoin-backed bonds
- Geothermal Bitcoin mining using volcano energy
- "Bitcoin City" concept at base of volcano
- Zero income, property, capital gains taxes for Bitcoin City

## The Reception

**Supporters Argue:**

- **Remittances**: 24% of El Salvador's GDP
  - Traditional: 7-10% fees via Western Union
  - Bitcoin: Near-zero fees via Lightning Network
  - $400M+ potential annual savings
- **Financial inclusion**: 70% of population unbanked
- **Tourism**: Bitcoin enthusiasts visit (Bitcoin Beach)
- **Foreign investment**: Crypto companies considering relocation

**Critics Point To:**

- Volatility risk for citizens' savings
- Technical challenges (app crashes on launch day)
- IMF pressure and loan negotiations
- Forced adoption concerns
- Limited actual usage after initial period
- Environmental concerns (mostly addressed)

## Government Bitcoin Holdings

President Bukele personally announces purchases on Twitter:

- Started accumulating June 2021
- Average purchases: 5-100 BTC
- Total holdings: **6,000+ BTC** (as of 2025)
- Purchased dip after dip
- Paper losses significant during 2022 bear market
- Substantial gains by 2024-2025 at $100k+

## Global Implications

El Salvador's experiment inspired:

- **Central African Republic** adopted Bitcoin (later reversed)
- **Prospera** in Honduras (special economic zone)
- Other developing nations studying model
- City-level adoption discussions worldwide

Despite implementation challenges, El Salvador proved a nation-state _could_ adopt Bitcoin as legal tender, survive, and potentially benefit. The experiment continues, providing real-world data on Bitcoin's viability as national currency.
