On July 22, 2014, **Ethereum launched its crowdsale**, selling ETH for Bitcoin at **$0.30 per ETH** and raising **31,591 BTC** (~$18 million), creating the **blueprint for ICOs**, funding the development of the **world's second-largest blockchain**, and minting **countless millionaires** among early believers who paid pennies for tokens worth thousands.

## The Crowdsale

**The Offer:**

- **Duration**: July 22 - September 2, 2014 (42 days)
- **Price**: 2,000 ETH per 1 BTC (declining scale)
  - First 14 days: 2,000 ETH/BTC (~$0.30 per ETH)
  - Next 14 days: Declining to 1,970 ETH/BTC
  - Final 14 days: Declining to 1,337 ETH/BTC
- **Raised**: 31,591 BTC (~$18.3 million at time)
- **ETH Created**: ~60 million for crowdsale participants
- **Total Genesis**: ~72 million ETH (60M sale + 12M foundation/dev)

**The Process:**

1. Send **Bitcoin** to crowdsale address
2. Receive **ETH** (delivered later when network launched)
3. Wait for **mainnet launch** (July 2015)
4. Proportional to amount/timing of contribution

**The Early Bird Bonus:**

- **First 2 weeks**: Best rate (2,000 ETH per BTC)
- **Linear decline**: After day 14
- **Final rate**: 1,337 ETH per BTC (leet number, intentional)
- **Incentive**: Buy early, get more ETH

## The Regulatory Question

**Was Ethereum ICO Legal?**

**Arguments It's a Security:**

- **Investment contract**: Money given expecting profit
- **Efforts of others**: Team building platform
- **Howey test**: Arguably fails it
- **SEC view**: Most ICOs are securities

**Arguments It's Not:**

- **Utility token**: Needed for gas/platform use
- **Decentralized**: No central issuer (eventually)
- **Swiss foundation**: Not US company
- **Pre-product sale**: Software license, not investment

**The SEC's Position:**

- **2018**: William Hinman (SEC) said ETH is **not a security** (currently)
- **Reasoning**: "Sufficiently decentralized"
- **Bitcoin exemption**: Also applies to ETH
- **Most other tokens**: Still securities

**The Luck:**

Ethereum launched **before** regulators understood crypto.

If it launched in 2018+, likely would be **shut down as unregistered security**.

**Timing was everything.**
