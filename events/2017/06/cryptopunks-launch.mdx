## The Drop

**What Were CryptoPunks?**

- **10,000 unique** 24x24 pixel characters
- **Algorithmically generated** with random traits
- **Stored on Ethereum** blockchain
- **Free to claim** (launched when ETH was ~$100)
- **One per wallet** initially
- Inspired by **London punk scene** and **cyberpunk**

**The Types:**

- **6,039 Male Punks**
- **3,840 Female Punks**
- **88 Zombie Punks**
- **24 Ape Punks**
- **9 Alien Punks** (rarest)

Each with **unique combination** of traits:

- Hats, glasses, cigarettes, earrings
- Mohawks, beanies, bandanas
- Wild hair, messy hair, straight hair
- 0-7 attributes per Punk

**The Claim:**

In June 2017, anyone could:

1. Connect Ethereum wallet
2. Claim a **free Punk**
3. Pay only gas fees (~$0.50)
4. Own a piece of history

**9,000+ claimed** in the first week.
**1,000 retained** by Larva Labs.

## The Creators: Larva Labs

**Who Made Them?**

- **Matt Hall** and **John Watkinson**
- Canadian software developers
- Based in New York
- Created **mobile apps** before crypto
- **Not** crypto natives initially

**The Inspiration:**

- **London punk scene** (1970s)
- **Cyberpunk** aesthetics (William Gibson novels)
- **Rare Pepe** trading cards (crypto meme project)
- **CryptoKitties** (same summer, independently)
- Wanted to experiment with **blockchain scarcity**

**The Technology:**

Initially used **ERC-20** (fungible token standard):

- Each Punk was **separate contract**
- Could only be transferred to one owner at a time
- **Not quite the right fit**

This inspired the **ERC-721** standard (NFT standard) which launched months later.

**The Business Model:**

There wasn't one. Initially:

- **Free to claim** (just gas)
- **No royalties** on secondary sales
- No monetization plan
- **Experiment** in digital scarcity

Larva Labs kept **1,000 Punks**. At peak, worth **$100M+**.

## The Technology

**The Original Contract:**

- Deployed **June 2017**
- Before ERC-721 standard existed
- Used **modified ERC-20** approach
- Images **not on chain** initially
- Metadata stored off-chain

**The V1 Bug:**

- Original contract had a **bug**
- Couldn't actually transfer Punks properly
- Had to **redeploy** (V2 contract)
- V1 Punks still exist (very rare, different addresses)

**On-Chain vs Off-Chain:**

- **Images**: Off-chain (IPFS/server)
- **Ownership**: On-chain
- **Metadata**: On-chain hash
- **Debate**: Not fully decentralized

**2021 Update:**

- Larva Labs made images **fully on-chain**
- All Punk images in contract storage
- More expensive gas, but **truly immutable**
- Silenced "not actually on chain" critics

## The Floor Wars

**What Is the Floor?**

- **Cheapest available Punk** on market
- Indicator of **minimum value**
- Watched obsessively by collectors
- **Status game**: Bragging rights

**The Floor Dynamics:**

- **Bull market**: Floor rises (more buyers)
- **Bear market**: Floor falls (fewer buyers)
- **Rares**: Trade at multiples of floor
- **Common traits**: Near floor
- **Rare traits**: 5-100x floor

**Floor Over Time:**

- **2017**: 0 (free)
- **2018**: 0.1 ETH
- **2019**: 0.5 ETH
- **2020**: 5 ETH
- **2021 Peak**: 150 ETH (~$500k)
- **2022-23 Bear**: 40-60 ETH
- **2024-26**: 30-80 ETH (fluctuates)

**The Psychology:**

Floor price = **minimum respect**.

If you can't afford floor, you're **not in the club**.

Brutal, but effective **scarcity mechanic**.

## The Yuga Labs Acquisition

**March 2022: Acquisition:**

- **Yuga Labs** (Bored Ape creators) acquired CryptoPunks
- Also acquired **Meebits** (Larva Labs 3D NFT project)
- **Undisclosed price** (likely $100M+)
- Larva Labs **exited** NFT space

**What Changed:**

1. **Commercial rights**: Granted to holders
2. **Integration**: With Bored Ape ecosystem
3. **Marketing**: More aggressive promotion
4. **Licensing**: Punks can be used commercially

**Before Yuga:**

- No commercial rights
- Larva Labs controlled IP
- Can't use your Punk commercially

**After Yuga:**

- Full commercial rights
- Use in merch, media, etc.
- Similar to Bored Apes model

**The Controversy:**

- **Some loved it**: More utility, better for holders
- **Some hated it**: Purists felt it "sold out"
- **Original vision**: Art project, not commercial empire
- **New reality**: Business asset
