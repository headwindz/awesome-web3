On December 18, 2017, **MakerDAO** launches **DAI**, the first decentralized, collateral-backed stablecoin on Ethereum. Unlike Tether's opaque reserves, DAI is fully transparent, overcollateralized with ETH, and governed by code. It becomes the foundation of DeFi.

## The Launch

**What Is DAI:**

- **$1 USD soft-pegged** stablecoin
- **Overcollateralized** by ETH (150%+ required)
- **Trustless** - no company holds reserves
- **Transparent** - all collateral on-chain
- **Decentralized** - governed by MKR token holders

**How It Works:**

1. Lock ETH in Maker Vault (formerly CDP)
2. Generate DAI (borrow against your ETH)
3. Minimum 150% collateralization
4. Pay stability fee (interest) in MKR
5. Return DAI + fee to unlock ETH

**Initial Parameters:**

- Collateral: ETH only (initially)
- Minimum ratio: 150%
- Stability fee: ~0.5% annually
- Price: $1.00 target
- Supply: Started small, grew to billions

## Why This Mattered

**The Stablecoin Problem:**

Before DAI:

- **Tether (USDT):** Opaque, centralized, questionable reserves
- **USDC/PAX:** Didn't exist yet
- **BitUSD:** Algorithmic, had issues
- No trustless, decentralized option

DAI offered:

- **Transparency:** All collateral visible on-chain
- **Decentralization:** No single point of failure
- **Overcollateralization:** Can't be undercollateralized
- **Governance:** Community controlled via MKR

**The DeFi Foundation:**

DAI became the building block for DeFi:

- **Compound:** Lend/borrow DAI
- **Uniswap:** Trade DAI pairs
- **Aave:** DAI markets
- **Curve:** DAI stablecoin pools
- Every DeFi protocol uses DAI

Without decentralized stablecoin, DeFi couldn't exist.

## How DAI Keeps $1 Peg

**The Mechanisms:**

**Price Too High (>$1):**

- Lower stability fee
- Cheaper to create DAI
- More DAI minted
- Supply increases
- Price falls to $1

**Price Too Low (<$1):**

- Raise stability fee
- Expensive to create DAI
- People close CDPs
- Supply decreases
- Price rises to $1

**Additional Tools:**

- **Dai Savings Rate (DSR):** Pays holders to lock DAI
- **Liquidations:** Under-collateralized vaults liquidated
- **Emergency shutdown:** If system fails, collateral returned
- **Peg Stability Module (PSM):** Trade USDC/DAI at $1

**The Reality:**

Mostly works:

- Usually within $0.98-$1.02
- Occasional depegs during volatility
- Black Thursday (March 2020) tested it
- Generally stable

## The Black Thursday Test (March 2020)

**The Crisis:**

March 12, 2020:

- COVID crash
- ETH drops 50% in hours ($200 â†’ $100)
- Gas prices surge to 500+ gwei
- Maker system breaks temporarily

**What Went Wrong:**

- Liquidations failed (gas too expensive)
- Keepers couldn't liquidate vaults fast
- Some vaults liquidated for $0 DAI
- $4-5M bad debt created
- System undercollateralized briefly

**The Response:**

- MKR dilution to cover bad debt
- $5.3M new MKR minted
- Debt auction cleared
- System recapitalized
- Proved governance works

**The Lesson:**

- System survived extreme stress
- Governance responded effectively
- But showed vulnerabilities
- Led to improvements (PSM, better oracles)
- Made DAI stronger

Is DAI really decentralized?

**Arguments For:**

**Arguments Against:**

**The Question:**

Is DAI now just USDC with extra steps?

## Lessons Learned

**1. Overcollateralization Works:**

More capital efficient? No. More stable? Yes. Trade-off worth it.

**2. Decentralization Has Limits:**

Pure decentralization (ETH only) didn't scale. Pragmatism (add USDC) did.

**3. Governance is Hard:**

Voter apathy, whale dominance, slow decisions. But it works.

**4. Black Swans Happen:**

March 2020 broke the system temporarily. But it recovered. Resilience matters.

**5. First Mover Advantage:**

DAI established itself as DeFi's stablecoin. Hard to displace.

## The Controversy

## The Final Word

MakerDAO and DAI represent DeFi's greatest success and biggest compromise. They proved you could create stable value from code, build transparent financial systems, and govern on-chain.

But they also showed that pure decentralization doesn't scale easily. Adding USDC collateral was practical but philosophically questionable. The trade-off between principles and growth is DeFi's eternal struggle.

**The lesson:** Perfect is the enemy of good. DAI isn't perfectly decentralized, but it's the best decentralized stablecoin we have. Sometimes "good enough" changes the world more than "perfect" theories.

Without DAI, there's no DeFi. That's the legacy. Everything else is details.

From zero to $10 billion in stable value, created by code and collateral. That's magic. Even if the magic has some centralized ingredients.
